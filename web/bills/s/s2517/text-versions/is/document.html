                                 A BILL


 
     To prohibit bonuses to senior-level IRS executives until all 
      Congressional requests for documents, including electronic 
   communications, related to the investigation of IRS targeting of 
                        taxpayers are complete.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the 
United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ``Holding the IRS Leadership 
Accountable Act''.

SEC. 2. PROHIBITION ON BONUSES UNTIL IRS COMPLIES WITH CONGRESSIONAL 
              DOCUMENT REQUESTS.

    The Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall not provide any 
performance award (including bonuses, step increases, and leave time) 
to senior-level executives of the Internal Revenue Service until such 
time as the Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration certifies 
that the Internal Revenue Service has provided all documents, including 
all electronic communications, requested by the relevant congressional 
committees in due course of their investigation into the targeting of 
certain taxpayers who applied for designation as an organization 
described in section 501(c)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.
