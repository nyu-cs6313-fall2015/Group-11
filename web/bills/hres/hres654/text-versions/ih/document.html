                               RESOLUTION


 
Relating to the application of Article II, section 3, clause 5, of the 
                   Constitution of the United States.

    Resolved, That the Secretary of Health and Human Services has 
failed to meet the requirement of Article II, section 3, clause 5, of 
the Constitution of the United States to take care that a law be 
faithfully executed, with respect to the policy of the Department of 
Health and Human Services providing for the authorization, approval, 
renewal, modification, or extension of any experimental, pilot, or 
demonstration project under section 1115 of the Social Security Act (42 
U.S.C. 1315) that waives compliance with a requirement of section 407 
of such Act (42 U.S.C. 607) through a waiver of section 402 of such Act 
(42 U.S.C. 602); and that the House of Representatives shall bring a 
civil action in the United States District Court for the District of 
Columbia for declaratory or injunctive relief to challenge the policy 
of the Secretary of Health and Human Services.
